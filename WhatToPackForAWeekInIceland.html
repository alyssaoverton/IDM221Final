What To Pack For A Week In Iceland

April 7, 2017

Before my study abroad trip to Reykjavik, the professor running the program told our entire group to pack and dress in layers. She also told us to pack fairly lightly, which REALLY confused me and made my packing that much worse. Typically, I like to consider myself a solid packer. I always do it last minute – as in a few hours before I have to leave. But somehow, I always manage to pack everything I need and a little more to be safe. However, for some reason I didn’t pack nearly enough clothes for this trip and to make things worse, the clothes I brought weren’t even warm enough to withstand Iceland’s crazy weather.

Since I’ve lived in Upstate New York my entire life, I know exactly what true cold weather feels like. I’ve dealt with the terrible snow storms, icy roads and chilling wind and somehow I was still unprepared for the weather I experienced during my week stay in Iceland. My first tip on packing would definitely be to bring as much waterproof gear as possible. And don’t even bother bringing an umbrella. None of the locals used umbrellas when I was there, and to be completely honest the wind is so strong some of the time it would just turn your umbrella inside out and drag you down the street. Anyways, waterproof clothing/coats are SO important because it rains so randomly in Iceland. It could be a bright, beautiful sunny day and in a matter of minutes it could start down pouring. I know this because it actually happened to me while walking to one of the Design March events. Yes, I showed up pretty damp and the walk there was completely miserable – but would have been a little better if the coat I had on was waterproof. I was wearing one of those knee-length down puffer coats that are pretty much worn only when genuinely necessary because its so freaking cold outside. If you don’t have a waterproof coat, at least find one that is water resistant and maybe invest in a waterproofing spray. I personally didn’t do this, but other people on my trip did. The next thing I will stress is to definitely dress in layers and do your best to overpack. When I was packing for this trip I was trying to pack “layers” while also packing light, which ended up in me packing a ton of light layers. The heavier & more layers you have the better, especially if you’re out sightseeing all day. You can always take layers off when the sun comes out and leave them in your car or on the tour bus. But even then, it may be good to pack extra layers in your bag just in case it gets colder throughout the day. Lastly, I think the most obvious important item to pack other than a coat is a few good pairs of shoes. I went on this trip mainly for Design March & the Reykjavik Fashion Festival so I had to have fashion in mind while packing. I brought black knee-high leather boots, a waterproof pair of hiking boots and an older pair of sneakers. I definitely wish I had brought one more pair of fashion boots, but like I said before I was trying to pack as light as possible. I used a water resistant lotion on my leather boots before leaving on the trip and they lasted fine the entire trip & did not get ruined so make sure if you have a nice pair of shoes you want to wear, weather proofing them is a must. Depending on what you’ll be doing during your stay in Iceland will determine if this article was actually helpful or not!! But if you’ve made it this far, I have compiled a list of Iceland essentials down below:

  • Extremely warm socks (tech/thermal, wool, fleece)
  • Waterproof jacket/coat
  • Hiking shoes and sneakers
  • Waterproofing spray
  • Gloves (preferably the tech ones so you can take photos on your phone without having to take your gloves off)
  • Sunglasses
  • Bathing suit (for the Blue Lagoon)
  • Granola bars (you will thank me later)
  • Portable charger (some of the touristy day trips last quite literally ALL day so its best to have one on hand)
  • An extra towel (Iceland is a very eco-friendly country, many of the hotels only give one towel per person and expect you to use the same one for 2-3 days, so bring an extra if you like to use more than one/prefer a fresher towel)
